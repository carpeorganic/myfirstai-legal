Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-05-30 · Last updated: 2026-05-30

Carpe (hereinafter the “Company”) values the privacy of its users and complies with the Personal Information Protection Act and other applicable laws. This policy explains how the MyFirstAI app and related services (the “Service”) handle personal information.

1. Personal Information Collected and How It Is Collected

The Company collects and uses the following items.

CategoryItemsMethodPurpose
Device & usage data (required) Advertising identifier (IDFA / Android Advertising ID), device model & OS version, app version, language/region settings, usage logs (access time, screen navigation, feature usage frequency), crash/error reports Generated and collected automatically when the app runs Maintaining service stability, diagnosing errors, analyzing usage statistics, improving features
Payment information (for in-app purchases) Transaction identifier (order number), product name, payment amount, payment date/time, receipt information (issued by Apple App Store / Google Play) Collected through the payment platform when the user makes an in-app purchase Payment verification, delivery of subscriptions/items, refund and dispute handling, accounting and tax processing
AI feature usage information Text, voice, and other inputs the user provides to the AI feature, and the AI response results Collected and transmitted when the user uses the AI feature Generating AI responses and improving service quality. Not stored on the Company’s servers; transmitted to an external AI service provider, after which the response is received and the data is immediately discarded.

Because the Service is provided without a sign-up process, the Company does not collect directly identifying information such as name, email, or phone number.

2. Retention and Use Period

  • Device & usage data: stored for up to 12 months from the date of collection, then automatically destroyed. De-identified aggregate data for statistical purposes may be retained without a time limit.
  • Payment information: retained for 5 years in accordance with the Act on Consumer Protection in Electronic Commerce (records of contracts or withdrawal of subscription).
  • AI feature usage information: discarded immediately after the AI response is generated. Not stored on the Company’s servers.

3. Provision to Third Parties

The Company does not provide users’ personal information to third parties beyond the scope stated in this policy, except in the following cases.

  • When the user has given prior consent
  • When required by law, or when an investigative agency requests it in accordance with the procedures prescribed by law for investigative purposes

4. Outsourcing of Personal Information Processing

For smooth provision of the Service, the Company outsources personal information processing tasks as follows.

ProcessorOutsourced taskItems processed
Apple Inc. / Google LLC In-app purchase processing, app distribution Transaction identifier, receipt information, advertising identifier
Apple Inc. (App Analytics) / Google LLC (Firebase, etc.) Crash reporting, usage statistics analysis Device model & OS, usage logs, crash/error information, advertising identifier
Google LLC (Gemini), fal.ai, Pollinations.ai AI response generation Text, voice, and other inputs the user provides to the AI feature

If a processor changes or the scope of outsourced work changes, the Company will provide notice through an amendment to this policy.

5. Transfer Abroad

Some processors (e.g., Apple Inc., Google LLC, external AI service providers) are located overseas or operate overseas servers. In such cases, the Company outsources only to operators with secure transfer and processing procedures in accordance with applicable law. The items, purpose, country, timing, and method of transfer are described in the “4. Outsourcing of Personal Information Processing” table above.

6. Users’ Rights and How to Exercise Them

Users may exercise the following rights at any time.

  • Request to access, correct, delete, or suspend the processing of personal information
  • Reset the advertising identifier or opt out of tracking (iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking → turn off Allow Apps to Request to Track / Android: Settings → Privacy → Ads → reset or delete advertising ID)
  • Request an in-app purchase refund (Apple App Store: reportaproblem.apple.com / Google Play: request from purchase history at play.google.com)

These rights may be exercised by contacting the email at the end of this policy, and the Company will act without delay.

7. Destruction Procedure and Method

Personal information whose retention period has elapsed or whose processing purpose has been achieved is destroyed without delay.

  • Electronic files: permanently deleted so they cannot be recovered or reproduced
  • Non-electronic forms such as printouts (if any): shredded or incinerated

8. Measures to Ensure Security

  • Administrative measures: establishment and implementation of an internal management plan, regular self-inspection
  • Technical measures: access control, encryption of transmission sections (HTTPS/TLS), intrusion prevention systems
  • Physical measures: access control to servers and data rooms where data is stored

9. Advertising Identifiers and Automatic Collection Tools

The Company may use OS-provided advertising identifiers (IDFA, AAID) for usage statistics analysis and ad identification purposes. Users may reset or opt out of advertising identifiers in their device settings.

10. Protection of Children Under 14

The Company does not actively market the Service to children under 14. If a child under 14 uses the Service, we recommend use with the consent of a legal guardian, who may request access, correction, deletion, or suspension of processing of the child’s personal information via the contact at the end of this policy.
